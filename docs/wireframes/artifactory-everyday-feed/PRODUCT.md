# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Everyday TinyCloud users who want a useful private feed made from their own
Listen context. They are here to read, ask, react, and understand why something
appeared—not to operate an agent framework or manage capability bundles.

## Product Purpose

Feed turns a user's private context into useful artifacts. One TinyCloud sign-in
establishes the authority chain from Feed to Feed Host to Artifactory and its
agent. Setup succeeds automatically or explains a concrete recovery action.
Success means the user reaches useful content without learning about bundles,
DIDs, manifests, delegation requests, or infrastructure boundaries.

## Brand Personality

Calm, legible, and trustworthy. The product should feel like Listen: direct
language, familiar controls, and quiet confidence around private data.

## Anti-references

- Developer consoles presented to everyday users.
- First-run screens that ask users to approve packages, bundles, hashes, or
  internal runtime policy.
- Repeated wallet or capability prompts after the initial sign-in.
- Opaque automation that cannot be inspected or paused when the user chooses.
- Dense dashboards that make the feed feel like an operations product.

## Design Principles

1. Sign in once; establish the normal delegation chain automatically.
2. Lead with the user's task—read, ask, save, hide—not system topology.
3. Show progress in plain language only while work is actually happening.
4. Keep authority invisible by default and fully inspectable from an optional
   Access & automation view.
5. Explain every artifact through human-readable source provenance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. All actions must be keyboard accessible, status changes must
be announced without stealing focus, touch targets must be at least 44 pixels,
and motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. Never rely on color alone
for status or authority state.
