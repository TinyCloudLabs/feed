# Product

## Register

product

## Users

Everyday TinyCloud users who want a useful private feed made from their own
context. They are here to scan short posts, expand the ones that matter, ask,
react, and understand why something appeared—not to operate an agent framework
or manage capability bundles.

## Product Purpose

Feed turns rich artifacts made from a user's private context into a familiar,
tweet-like stream of useful Feed posts. A rich artifact may contribute several
independently ranked posts; expanding any post reveals the shared artifact and,
when available, the section that supports that post. Artifact-making
workflows are intentionally varied: the early intelligence workflows are
examples, not the product boundary.

Success means users reach useful insights without learning about bundles,
DIDs, manifests, delegation requests, or infrastructure boundaries, while
artifact provenance and access remain inspectable.

## Brand Personality

Calm, legible, and trustworthy. The product should feel like Listen: direct
language, familiar controls, and quiet confidence around private data.

## Anti-references

- Developer consoles presented to everyday users.
- Dense dashboards that make the feed feel like an operations product.
- A feed where every artifact is forced into one oversized card.
- Opaque automation that cannot be inspected or paused when the user chooses.
- Repeated wallet or capability prompts after the initial sign-in.

## Design Principles

1. Lead with the Feed post; let the full artifact reward expansion.
2. Rank and react to posts independently without losing their artifact context.
3. Let many kinds of workflows and artifacts participate in one coherent feed.
4. Keep authority invisible by default and fully inspectable on demand.
5. Explain every post through human-readable artifact and source provenance.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

Meet WCAG 2.2 AA. All actions must be keyboard accessible, status changes must
be announced without stealing focus, touch targets must be at least 44 pixels,
and motion must respect reduced-motion preferences. Never rely on color alone
for status or authority state.
